1. The "Whale" Identification (High-Impact Analysis)
In the gaming industry, a small percentage of "Super-Hits" (Whales) drive a massive portion of total market revenue.

Task: Isolate and analyze all titles that achieved over 20 million units in global sales.

Objective: Identify commonalities among these outliers. Is success driven by specific publishers, or was it a product of a specific era?

2. Cultural Preference Mapping (NA vs. JP)
We cannot treat the global market as a single block. We need to verify the hypothesis that tastes in North America (NA) and Japan (JP) are fundamentally different.

Task: Perform a comparative analysis of Genre popularity between the NA and JP regions.

Deliverable: A grouped bar chart showing side-by-side sales performance for each genre in these two regions.

3. Platform Efficiency Study (The Profitability Trap)
Total sales figures can be misleading. A console may have high total sales simply because it had a massive library of thousands of games.

Task: Calculate the Average (Mean) Sales per Game for every platform in the dataset.

Objective: Identify the platform with the highest "Success Probability" per title—where a single release has the best statistical chance of becoming a bestseller.

4. The "Market Pulse" Timeline
We need to understand the lifecycle of the physical software market to determine when the industry reached its peak and how it has shifted since.

Task: Generate a timeline visualization of Total Global Sales per year (1980–2020).

Objective: Define the "Golden Era" of peak revenue and assess the current trajectory of physical game sales.
